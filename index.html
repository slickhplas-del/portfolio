Who I Am

The Problem
I Was Built to Solve

Most companies hire a marketing manager after the function already exists. Someone built the brand, set the strategy, hired the team, created the tools and processes — and now they need someone to manage what's already there.

That's not where I've spent my career.

Every significant role I've held started with the same situation: a company with ambition, a social media page, and no real marketing function. No brand identity. No target audience defined. No team. No systems. Sometimes no budget clarity.

I walk in, diagnose the gap between where the business is and where leadership wants it to go, build the infrastructure to get there, hire and develop the people to run it, and create the systems that keep it producing when I'm not in the room.

I've done this three times. The function existed after I left. That's the metric that matters.

What I find when I arrive

Active social pages with no brand identity. No tone of voice. No target audience defined. No performance marketing. No lead generation system. No reporting. A CEO with goals and no roadmap.

What I leave behind

A brand foundation. A hired and trained team. A ClickUp-based operating system. A reporting structure. A campaign pipeline. A department that functions without me in every decision.

03
Methodology

How I Build a
Marketing Department

Developed across three full department builds. Repeatable. Documented. The goal is always a function that produces results without needing me in every decision.

WEEK 1
Diagnose
Meet the CEO and business development lead. Extract the real vision. Map the competitive landscape. Audit everything that exists — pages, content, prior campaigns. Identify what's broken and what's missing entirely.
WEEKS 2–4
Build the Foundation
Define brand purpose, mission, values, target audience, tone of voice, key messages, visual identity — colors, fonts, graphic elements. Everything the team uses as their north star before a single ad runs.
MONTH 2
Build the Team & Systems
Hire for outcomes, not roles. Implement ClickUp: task assignment, review stages, reporting, attendance. Brief every team member with quarterly content plans. Cross-train deliberately — pair strong performers with developing ones.
MONTH 3+
Execute & Scale
Launch performance campaigns with A/B testing. Weekly reports upward to leadership. Man-to-man sessions with each team member. The department runs when I'm not in the room. That's when it's done.

The 10-Day Crisis

What Was at Stake

A major US solar sales account was at risk of being lost due to insufficient qualified agents. In a leadership meeting the CEO stated plainly: if headcount isn't filled in 10 days, the company closes. Forty-five people's livelihoods were on the line. An external recruiter had been quoted — EGP 2,000 per hire, two-week timeline. They produced zero results.

What I Did — Unasked

Recruitment was not my job. I raised my hand in the meeting. I knew how to bring people through performance marketing — I'd done it for clients. I asked for the requirements and ran the campaign myself. Built the ad, defined the audience, set the budget, monitored daily. Applied the same logic I used to generate real estate leads to generate qualified job applicants.

Beyond the Crisis

The excess applicants — 160+ beyond what was needed — were archived into a structured backup talent pipeline that HR continued drawing from for months. Separately designed and delivered a 30-hour personal marketing course to 30 agents — reducing turnover by giving stuck employees a sense of growth and forward momentum.
